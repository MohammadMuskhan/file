A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India & People's President

Early Life

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble Tamil Muslim family. Despite financial hardships, he pursued his passion for flight and science, selling newspapers to fund his education.

Scientific Contributions

Dr. Kalam was a key figure in India's space and missile programs. He led the development of SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle, and played a pivotal role in projects like Agni and Prithvi missiles, earning him the title "Missile Man of India."

"Dream is not that which you see while sleeping; it is something that does not let you sleep."

Presidency & Legacy

Serving as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, Kalam was known as the "People's President." He inspired millions through his books like "Wings of Fire" and his vision for a developed India by 2020. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture.
